TPU8 is optimized to reduce training time for trillion parameter frontier models, delivering a staggering 121 exoflops of native FP4 computes and two pabytes of shared HPM within a single 9,600 chip super pod. A 3x increase in peak performance per super pod over the previous generation with 2x ICI scaleup bandwidth and four times raw scale out VCN bandwidth compared to the previous generation. TPU8 drastically reduces data bottlenecks. To further accelerate the development of Frontier models, we are scaling distributed training beyond a single superb pod. Utilizing pathways in conjunction with the Jax development framework, we can now scale to over 1 million TPU chips in a single training cluster. Within the cluster, up to 134,000 chips have non-blocking communication, delivering 1.6 6 million exoflops with nearlinear scaling performance using Virgo, our brand new state-of-the-art networking fabric. To effectively scale data heavy workloads, we've eliminated the training delays caused by data ingestion bottlenecks. By integrating manage luster, now with an industry-leading 10 terabytes per second of throughput and TPU direct, we deliver 10x faster storage bandwidth and directto chip RDMA access. TPU8 redefineses performance capability by moving block scale multiplication directly inside the MXUS, eliminating VPU overhead and enabling higher quality, low precision flops through native in MXU quantization by supporting smaller block sizes. TPU8 maintains superior model quality while staying perfectly balanced across all arithmetic and network intensities. This architectural harmony minimizes exposed vector ops time through balanced VPU scaling and finer grained offloads to secondary threads. Ultimately, by unbburdening the primary tensor core and easing pressure on collective communication overlap, TPU8 allows for more flexible partitioning strategies that push the absolute limits of model flops utilization at massive scale. Whether you are training a multi- trillion parameter model or performing bulk inference, TPU8 provides the raw arithmetic density and bisection bandwidth needed to push the boundaries of AI pre-training. As we enter the agentic era, the industry is hitting what we call the latency wall where traditional architectures struggle with the real-time demands of auto reggressive decoding and complex chain of thought reasoning models. That is why alongside TPU8, I am also thrilled to announce a second eighth gen TPU that directly addresses this issue. TPU 8i is our specialized post-training and inference engine. While it remains highly capable of training any state-of-the-art frontier model, we are optimizing its core features to be the best reinforcement learning and serving infrastructure for the next generation of reasoning models. A defining breakthrough for TPU 8i lies in its ICI networking architecture which pioneers the boardfly topology for TPUs. Moving beyond our standard 3D Taurus mesh, this hierarchical boardfly design maintains a compact network diameter to reduce tail latency for the complex alltoall collective communications that drive the next generation of mixture of experts models by shortening the network diameter needed for all toall communication. the very heartbeat of reasoning models. Boardfly achieves up to a 50% improvement in latency for communication intensive workloads. TPU 8i also breaks the memory wall with 3x more onchip SRAMM over the previous generation to host a larger KV cache entirely on silicon, significantly reducing the idle time of the cores during long context decoding. By integrating a specialized collectives acceleration engine, TPU8i further reduces the onchip latency of collectives by 5x. Lower latency per collective operation means less time spent waiting, directly contributing to higher throughput required to run millions of concurrent agents instantly. The result is a system that delivers 80% better performance per dollar for low latency serving compared to previous generations.
